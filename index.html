Representative creative works

1. Elegy for Strings (2025)

~6 mins — String Orchestra

Score (PDF) · Recording (MP3)

*Elegy for Strings* unfolds as a meditation on resonance, memory, and suspended harmonic motion. Built around slowly evolving modal inflections and overlapping timbral layers, the piece explores long-form phrase shaping through delicate registral shifts and subtle harmonic rotations. The writing privileges inwardness and restraint: tension accumulates through density rather than volume, and release arrives only through the gradual rebalancing of spectral weight. It is music that searches gently, holding space for reflection.

2. Codex (2024)

~5 mins — Solo Piano

Score (PDF)

*Codex* is built from a small collection of generative harmonic cells that recombine and mutate through algorithmic procedures. Its form unfolds like a manuscript being assembled and erased in real time: patterns emerge, fracture, and reappear transformed. The work balances an austere structural logic with moments of spontaneous lyricism, reflecting my interest in how rule-based processes intersect with embodied performance and intuition.

3. Ave Regina caelorum (2023)

~4 mins — Chamber Choir

Score (PDF)

*Ave Regina caelorum* reimagines the antiphon as a series of luminous contrapuntal strands that drift between clarity and obscurity. Modal inflections anchor the work historically, while the harmonic language stretches toward spectral and post-tonal colors. The piece is deliberately spacious, allowing individual lines to breathe while building a collective resonance that feels both ancient and immediate.
